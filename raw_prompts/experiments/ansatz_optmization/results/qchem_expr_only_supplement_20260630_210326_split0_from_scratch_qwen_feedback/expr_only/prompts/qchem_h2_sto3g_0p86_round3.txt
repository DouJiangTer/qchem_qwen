- Expressibility E is an absolute score exp(-D_KL). The evaluator samples random parameter vectors uniformly from [-pi, pi], computes pairwise state fidelities, compares their histogram with the Haar fidelity distribution using KL divergence D_KL, and returns exp(-D_KL). Higher is better.
- Trainability T is an absolute barren-plateau proxy from Hamiltonian-energy parameter-shift gradients. The evaluator samples random parameter vectors, computes energy gradients with +/- pi/2 shifts for up to 24 parameters, estimates gradient variance, then maps log10(variance) to a [0, 1] score. - Reliability R is an absolute estimated success probability. The evaluator transpiles the circuit to basis gates [rz, sx, x, cx] on a line coupling map, counts one-qubit and two-qubit gates, then scores (1 - 1e-3)^one_qubit_gates * (1 - 1e-2)^two_qubit_gates. Higher means fewer/noisier-device-costly gates after transpilation.
- Total is the simple mean (E + T + R) / 3 for correct circuits; incorrect circuits receive zero scores.
